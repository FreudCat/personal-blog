Pet Emergency Clinic: Flipped Classroom

Purpose: When I was asked to provide a lecture for continuing education hours for the technicians at my emergency/specialty center, I felt that this would be a great opportunity to utilize a flipped classroom approach with an emphasis on peer teaching. Here, I will describe my rationale and how I structured the session.

Deciding on a Topic

I decided the topic should be one that could be immediately applied, could assist in patient care, and could support the other specialties in the hospital.

My audience would be licensed veterinary technicians who work in the hospital.

After polling the technicians who would be attending the continuing education session, I decided that we would practice placing Peripherally Inserted Central Catheter (PICC) lines.



Designing the Presentation

The learning objectives included:

  • Visualize and understand the purpose of a multi-lumen PICC line
  • Be able to place the PICC line in either the jugular vein or the lateral saphenous vein
  • Understand how to sample from the line using the three-syringe technique
